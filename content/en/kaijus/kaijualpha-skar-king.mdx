<Section icon={Swords} title="Comprehensive Moveset and Damage Analysis">

Understanding the damage output of the **kaijualpha skar king** is crucial for winning trades. His kit is built around the Bone Whip, which offers surprising range for a melee-focused game. Below is a detailed breakdown of his primary abilities based on base stats.

| Move Number | Ability Name | Damage (Base) | Energy Cost | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Move 1** | Bone Whip | 500 - 525 | Low | Primary poke move with exceptional range. |
| **Move 2** | Punch | 325 - 410 | Very Low | Fast close-quarters strike for finishing combos. |
| **Move 3** | Crystal Stab | 395 | Medium | Slow wind-up, difficult to land, moderate damage. |
| **Move 4** | Whip Ground Slam | 1000+ (Combo) | Medium | High impact move that slams the whip down. |

The **Crystal Stab** (Move 3) is often criticized by the community for its slow animation and relatively low damage compared to the Whip. In most combat scenarios, it is more efficient to cycle between the Whip and the Ground Slam to maintain a safe distance while chipping away at the opponent's health.

<Callout type="warning" title="Combat Warning">
The Crystal Stab takes a long time to land. If you miss, you leave yourself wide open to high-damage counters like Atomic Breaths or Pulses. Use it only when the opponent is stunned or distracted.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Optimal Skill Point Distribution">

To make the most of the **kaijualpha skar king**, you need to allocate your skill points effectively. Unlike tanks that dump everything into HP, Skar King benefits from a balanced build that emphasizes his hit-and-run nature.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Strength">
    Invest heavily in Strength first. This increases your Whip damage from 500 to over 600, making every poke significantly more threatening.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Invest in Regeneration">
    Add at least 5-10 points into Regen. Since Skar King needs to weave in and out of combat, being able to recover health while kiting is essential.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Balance Health (HP)">
    Aim for approximately 4,800 to 5,000 HP. This gives you enough cushion to survive a few mistakes without making you too slow or weak in the damage department.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Energy Optimization">
    If you find yourself relying on the Shimo summon, put a few points into Energy to speed up the generation process during combat.
  </Step>
</Steps>
